  TRANSFORMER VS CNN ANALYSIS:
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  1. Transformers need MORE data than CNNs
     - DeiT-Base: 86M parameters vs EfficientNetB2: ~9M
     - Stronger inductive bias in CNNs helps small datasets

  2. Pretraining is critical for transformers on small data
     - DeiT uses ImageNet-1k pretrained weights
     - Still insufficient for full fine-tuning

  3. Training time disadvantage
     - Transformers are computationally heavier
     - Attention mechanism O(n²) complexity

  4. Performance trade-off
     - Transformers: Better accuracy/F1, worse AUC/time
     - CNNs: Better AUC/time, slightly lower accuracy/F1
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  RECOMMENDATIONS TO BEAT CNNs:
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  1. Test-Time Augmentation (TTA)
     - Expected: +0.005-0.01 AUC gain
     - No training cost

  2. Increase resolution to 384×384
     - Expected: +0.003-0.008 AUC gain
     - +30% training time

  3. Stratified 5-Fold Ensemble
     - Expected: +0.005-0.015 AUC gain
     - 5× training time (~9 hours)

  4. Stronger augmentation (MixUp, CutMix)
     - Expected: +0.002-0.005 AUC gain
     - Moderate training cost

  5. Reduce overfitting (lower dropout, higher weight decay)
     - Expected: Better generalization
     - Minimal training cost
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  CONCLUSION:
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  DeiT-Base demonstrates competitive performance on the
  BreakHis breast cancer dataset, achieving the highest
  accuracy (95.01%) and F1 score (0.9640) among all models.

  However, it does not surpass EfficientNetB2 in AUC-ROC
  (0.9721 vs 0.9862) and requires 9× more training time.

  For this small medical imaging dataset, CNNs remain the
  superior choice due to:
  - Better AUC-ROC performance
  - Faster training
  - Stronger inductive bias for small data

  Transformers show promise but require larger datasets or
  ensemble techniques to consistently outperform CNNs.

  🚨 Research prototype. Not for clinical use.
